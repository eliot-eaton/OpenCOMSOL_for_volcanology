# OpenCOMSOL for Volcanology

A collaborative repository for sharing COMSOL Multiphysics models related to volcanic processes and volcanology research.

## üåã About

This repository serves as a community hub for volcanologists and computational scientists to share, collaborate on, and discuss COMSOL models for volcanic systems. Whether you have published models, works in progress, or just ideas for future modeling efforts, this is the place to contribute!

## üìÇ Repository Structure

The repository is organized into three main sections:

### [Published Models](./published_models/)
Peer-reviewed and published COMSOL models associated with scientific publications. These models are complete, validated, and ready for use by the community.

### [Unfinished Models](./unfinished_models/)
Work-in-progress models currently under development. These models may be seeking feedback, collaboration, or are in various stages of completion.

### [Model Ideas](./model_ideas/)
Concepts and proposals for future COMSOL models. Share your research questions, discuss modeling approaches, and find collaborators.

## üöÄ Getting Started

### For Users
1. Browse the directories to find models of interest
2. Read the README in each model directory for specific information
3. Download model files (.mph) and associated documentation
4. Refer to the original publications for theoretical background

### For Contributors
1. Review our [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md) guidelines
2. Choose the appropriate directory for your contribution
3. Follow the directory-specific submission guidelines
4. Submit a pull request or contact the maintainers

## üî¨ Types of Models

This repository welcomes models related to (but not limited to):
- Magma chamber mechanics and deformation
- Volcanic conduit dynamics
- Lava flow emplacement
- Pyroclastic density currents
- Volcanic degassing and gas transport
- Hydrothermal systems
- Caldera formation and collapse
- Volcanic edifice stability
- Thermal evolution of volcanic systems
- Multi-phase flow in volcanic settings
- Coupled magma-hydrothermal systems

## ü§ù Community Guidelines

- **Be respectful**: This is a collaborative scientific community
- **Give credit**: Always cite original work and acknowledge contributors
- **Share openly**: Follow open science principles when possible
- **Help others**: Provide constructive feedback and assistance
- **Document thoroughly**: Well-documented models are more valuable to the community

## üìã Requirements

- COMSOL Multiphysics (version should be specified in each model's README)
- Appropriate COMSOL modules for specific models
- Basic understanding of volcanic processes and numerical modeling
